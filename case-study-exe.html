Case Study: EXE Platform – Electrical Layout Documentation

Technical Author | High-NA EUV Systems | :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Overview

Contributed to documentation for the EXE platform, a next-generation High-NA EUV system, focusing on electrical layout procedures. The project required high precision, fast turnaround, and alignment with evolving engineering requirements.

Problem

The project required creating and updating complex procedures with minimal tolerance for error. Documentation had to align precisely with electrical systems while supporting multiple review layers including technical and safety validations.

Additionally, the fast-paced environment required reducing errors and improving turnaround time compared to previous project cycles.

Approach

  • Applied learnings from previous NXE projects to reduce documentation errors
  • Collaborated closely with Manufacturing Engineers and SMEs
  • Followed structured workflow: creation → self-review → quality → technical → safety review
  • Focused on clarity, consistency, and compliance with standards

Execution

Delivered high-quality procedures for electrical layouts by ensuring alignment with system requirements and maintaining strict adherence to documentation standards.

Actively participated in peer, technical, and safety reviews, contributing to improved overall documentation quality and consistency.

Impact

Near-Zero Errors

Achieved high accuracy in documentation delivery

High Demand Contributor

Retained in project due to consistent quality and reliability

Cross-Team Trust

Recognized by engineers and reviewers for clarity and precision

Key Learnings

  • Handling high-complexity systems with precision
  • Improving accuracy under tight timelines
  • Strengthening cross-functional collaboration
  • Adapting quickly to evolving technical requirements

Outcome

This project strengthened my ability to deliver high-accuracy documentation in complex engineering environments, while contributing effectively to system-level workflows and cross-functional teams.
